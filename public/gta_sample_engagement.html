What a Technology Assessment Looks Like in Practice
The following describes a representative 90-day engagement the type of work, the deliverables, and the outcomes. Client details have been generalized.
Engagement typeFixed-fee assessment
Duration90 days
OrganizationRegional healthcare nonprofit, 300+ staff
SituationPost-EHR transition, rapid growth
The situation

The organization had recently completed a major EHR transition, opened a new campus, and was pursuing a significant certification that would reshape how it delivered care. Leadership was capable and committed but the pace of change had outrun their ability to document it. Technology decisions were being made without a shared picture of where things stood, and no single person had a clear view of the full environment. The question was not whether to invest in technology it was where to focus first.

What the engagement delivered
1
Technology landscape document
A written inventory of every system in use what it does, how it connects to other systems, where the known gaps and pain points are, and what the organization does and does not control. Leadership described it as the first time they had a single, accurate picture of their technology environment.
2
Compliance and security review
A structured review of the organization's posture across HIPAA, applicable privacy regulations, and disaster recovery and business continuity. Delivered as a gap inventory with a prioritized action list a practical assessment of where the organization stood and what a reasonable path forward looked like.
3
IT team and organizational review
Structured conversations with each member of the IT team to understand workload, capacity, strengths, and gaps. The review surfaced role clarity issues and structural adjustments that could make the existing team more effective without adding headcount.
4
Executive priorities map
Individual conversations with each executive to understand what they needed most from technology in their own words. The process surfaced alignment and misalignment that had been implicit but never documented, giving the leadership team a shared language for technology conversations.
5
18-month technology roadmap
A practical roadmap organized into near-term actions the existing team could begin immediately, medium-term initiatives addressing the most important gaps, and longer-term investments requiring additional planning. Designed to be realistic and executable from day one.
